One process, multiple RF/GNSS sensor sources.

This demonstrator shows the patent discussion version of LANTERN: sensor feeds are normalised into a common evidence model, quality-gated, analysed for GNSS/RF burden, then converted into traceable launch-window, serviceability and site-selection outputs.

MOTH is an embodiment The process also accepts compatible spectrum scanners, SDR receivers, GNSS monitors or future EEI sensors.
Decision support only Outputs do not authorise flight, certify GNSS integrity or attribute interference to an actor.

Patent Conversation Cards

Does it require MOTH?

No. MOTH is the current embodiment. The claimed process can accept compatible RF/GNSS telemetry from alternative scanners, SDRs, receivers or future EEI sensors.

What is the technical contribution?

Different sensor logs are converted into a common evidence model, filtered for measurement quality, then scored against GNSS/RF operating conditions and field decision outputs.

What should be protected?

The sensor-agnostic evidence pipeline, quality/confidence handling, RF/GNSS burden assessment, launch-window ranking, site ranking and traceable operator reporting.
